Cover Letter Framework — Resume Builder Skill
==============================================
Use this template as the structural foundation. Every bracketed placeholder
must be replaced with specific, concrete details. Never use generic filler.
==============================================


SUBJECT LINE (for email applications):
Application for [Exact Job Title] — [Your Full Name]


---


[Your Name]
[City, State] | [Email] | [Phone] | [LinkedIn URL]

[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name or "Hiring Team"]
[Company Name]
[Company City, State] (optional)


---


OPENING PARAGRAPH — "Why this company and this role"
------------------------------------------------------
Rules:
- Reference something specific about the company (a product, a recent launch, a public
  mission statement, a market position) — show you did research
- State the role title exactly as written in the job posting
- Lead with your strongest relevant credential in one clause
- Length: 3–4 sentences

Template:
[Specific observation about the company that genuinely excites you] — this is why I'm
applying for the [Exact Job Title] role. With [X years] of experience [most relevant
credential], I've [one-sentence framing of your fit for this specific challenge].

Example:
"After watching [Company]'s shift from a product-led to an enterprise motion over the
past two years, I know exactly how hard that transition is — and I know I can accelerate
it. I'm applying for the Senior Product Manager, Enterprise role. After leading [Company]'s
SMB-to-mid-market transition that grew ARR from $4M to $12M in 18 months, I've built
the playbook this role requires."


---


BODY PARAGRAPH 1 — Most Relevant Achievement
---------------------------------------------
Rules:
- Pick your single most relevant accomplishment for THIS role
- Use the CAR framework: Challenge → Action → Result
- Include at least one hard metric
- Connect the achievement to a specific requirement from the job description
- Length: 3–5 sentences

Template:
At [Previous Company], I [describe the challenge or context]. I [specific action you
took — the approach, decision, or method]. As a result, [quantified outcome]. This
directly maps to [specific requirement or goal from the job description].

Example:
"At [Company], our checkout conversion was 12% below industry benchmarks, and the
engineering team had no visibility into which friction points mattered most. I ran
27 A/B tests over two quarters using a prioritization framework I built around impact
× confidence × effort. Checkout conversion increased by 34%, adding $8.2M in annual
revenue. I expect to bring this same structured experimentation approach to [Company]'s
growth challenges."


---


BODY PARAGRAPH 2 — Second Relevant Achievement or Complementary Strength
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rules:
- Address a second key requirement from the job description
- Or demonstrate a skill that paragraph 1 didn't cover
- Keep metrics if available; use scope/scale if no hard number
- Length: 3–4 sentences

Template:
[Brief context framing]. I [action or approach]. [Outcome or impact]. [One sentence
connecting it to the company's situation or this role's requirements.]


---


CLOSING PARAGRAPH — Call to Action
-----------------------------------
Rules:
- Express genuine enthusiasm — not "I would be a great fit" (everyone says this)
- State what you bring to this specific team or problem
- Clear, direct ask: an interview or a conversation
- Include your availability or response preference
- Length: 2–3 sentences maximum

Template:
[Company]'s focus on [specific challenge or opportunity] is exactly where I want to
be spending the next chapter of my career. I'd welcome the chance to walk you through
[specific aspect of your experience most relevant to their challenge] and discuss how
I can contribute. I'm available for a conversation at your convenience — [email] or
[phone].


---


SIGN-OFF:
[Sincerely / Best regards / Thank you],

[Your Full Name]


==============================================
CHECKLIST BEFORE SENDING
==============================================
[ ] Company name is spelled correctly everywhere
[ ] Job title matches the posting exactly
[ ] At least one specific company detail (not "I admire your innovative culture")
[ ] At least one hard metric in body paragraph 1
[ ] No phrase: "I am a results-driven professional"
[ ] No phrase: "I am passionate about..."
[ ] No phrase: "I believe I would be a great fit"
[ ] Total length: fits comfortably on one page (3–4 short paragraphs)
[ ] Greeting uses hiring manager's name if available
[ ] Subject line includes exact job title
