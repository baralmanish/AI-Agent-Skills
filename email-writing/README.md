# Email Writing Skill - Complete Guide

## Overview

**Email Writing Skill** transforms email intentions into polished, context-appropriate communication that achieves your goal. This skill adapts tone, formality, length, and psychological framing based on your purpose and recipient, then delivers multiple draft options.

---

## 🎯 What This Skill Does

Instead of staring at a blank email, you tell the AI:

- **Who** you're writing to
- **Why** you're writing
- **What** you want to happen
- **How** you want to sound

The AI generates:

1. **Primary draft** (recommended version)
2. **Two alternatives** (more formal / more casual)
3. **Three subject line options**
4. **Tone analysis** (formality level, emotional tone)
5. **Recipient psychology brief** (what they care about, objections, what wins them)
6. **Editing checklist** (quality checks before sending)
7. **Success metrics** (how to know if it worked)

### Full-Stack Developer Example: Three Contextual Emails in One Week

```
Scenario: You're a full-stack engineer with three emails to write:

1. Leadership update on security architecture implementation progress
   → Purpose: Status update to Engineering Director (metrics-first, concise)

2. Team announcement of new caching feature release
   → Purpose: Feature launch to peer engineers (collaborative, celebratory)

3. Reply to conference organizer with speaking bio and abstract
   → Purpose: Speaker credentials for keynote on service-oriented architecture
   → Want to establish credibility while keeping it engaging
```

**What the Skill Does:**

Each email gets **adapted to its specific context and recipient:**

- **Leadership email**: Headlines first, metrics before narrative, structured with bullets, clear blocker acknowledgment, solution-oriented tone
- **Team email**: Celebratory tone, benefit-focused, recognizes contributors, collaborative framing, makes change feel like shared win
- **Speaking email**: Professional credentials established, compelling talk angle, specific learnings from experience, unique perspective (learnings from failures)

**Why This Works:**

Rather than using generic templates, the same person gets three emails that are:

- ✅ Tonally appropriate to the recipient (executive, peer, professional audience)
- ✅ Structurally optimized (bullets for executives, narrative for team, credentials for speakers)
- ✅ Purposefully framed (results for leadership, enablement for team, expertise for conference)
- ✅ All authentically their voice while adapting to context

## 🚀 How to Use This Skill

### Step 1: Tell the AI Your Email Situation

Provide:

- **Recipient**: Who + title + relationship (if relevant)
- **Purpose**: What do you want to happen?
- **Key message**: 1-2 things they must know
- **Tone preference**: Formal? Warm? Casual? Urgent?
- **Context**: Relationship history, recipient's situation

### Step 2: AI Analyzes & Drafts

AI will:

- Identify the context (which of 11 types)
- Analyze recipient psychology
- Choose tone & formality level
- Structure email for maximum impact
- Generate 3 draft versions

### Step 3: Choose & Refine

You get multiple options:

- **Primary**: AI's recommended version
- **Alt A**: More formal
- **Alt B**: More casual/warm
- **Subject lines**: 3 options

Pick what feels right, tweak if needed, send.

## 🎓 Context-Specific Tips

### Leadership Emails

- **Lead with headline**: Status/result first, details second
- **Use structure**: Status | Wins | Challenges | Next Steps
- **Be honest**: Show problems + solutions
- **Respect time**: 3-4 paragraphs max
- **Include metrics**: Numbers before narrative

### Networking Emails

- **Personalize specifically**: Show you know their work
- **Lead with value to THEM**: Not what you want
- **Make specific ask**: Not vague "let's connect"
- **Respect their time**: Suggest specific format
- **Make saying yes easy**: Clear next step

### Difficult/Apology Emails

- **Lead with acknowledgment**: Not excuses
- **Take responsibility**: Specifically
- **Show you understand impact**: Not minimizing
- **Offer concrete fix**: Clear forward path
- **End on momentum**: Not dwelling on mistake

### Sales/Partnership Emails

- **Lead with THEIR problem**: Not your solution
- **Show you understand context**: Specific knowledge
- **Provide proof points**: Credibility matters
- **Make ask crystal clear**: Meeting? Pilot? Contract?
- **Reduce perceived risk**: Clear next step

## 📝 Examples

### Example 1: Quick Networking Reach Out

**User Input:**

```
Director at [Company], I've admired their work on [initiative].
First time reaching out. I want to learn from them. Keep it brief and warm.
```

**Primary Draft:**

```
Subject: Your talk on [Topic] really resonated with me

stuck with me: [quote or idea]. I'm building [your work] and trying to solve
[related challenge]. Curious if you've tackled this before.

Would love 20 minutes of your thinking if you're open to it. I'm happy to work
around your calendar.

Thanks for considering,
[Your name]
```

**Tone Card:**

- Formality: Professional-Warm (not too formal, genuine)
- Emotional tone: Earnest, respectful

---

### Example 2: Difficult Decline

**User Input:**

```
Peer who asked me to take on their project. We have good relationship.
I genuinely want to help but I'm overcommitted. Be kind but clear.
```

**Primary Draft:**

```
Subject: Re: That project you mentioned

Hey [Name],

I really appreciate you thinking of me for this. It's exactly the kind of work
I'd normally jump on, and I genuinely wish I had the capacity.

Here's my honest situation: I'm at maximum load on [Current project] through
[date]. Taking anything else on would mean doing both poorly.

What I CAN do: I can intro you to [Person] who might be perfect for this, or
I can pair with you for [specific limited help]. Either one?

Let me know what would be most useful.

[Your name]
```

**Recipient Psychology:**

- They likely feel: Hopeful you'll help
- Possible reaction: Disappointment, then appreciation for honesty
- What wins them: Clear alternative + genuine offer to help in another way

## ✅ Success Checklist

Before sending, verify:

- ✅ Purpose is clear (what do you want them to do?)

- ✅ Tone matches your relationship + the context

- ✅ CTA is crystal clear (if there is one)
- ✅ Email respects their time

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Lead with their perspective**: Start with what they care about, not what you want
2. **Specificity beats generality**: "Your talk on AI agents" beats "Your great work"
3. **CTA should be obvious**: "Reply by Friday" or "Let me know if you're interested"
4. **Tone > perfection**: A warm, slightly imperfect email often works better than a sterile perfect one
5. **Subject lines matter**: Make it clear why they should open (benefit, curiosity, or urgency)
6. **Respect their time**: State how long this will take or what you need
7. **One ask per email**: Multiple asks = lower response rate
8. **Timing matters**: Monday morning vs. Friday afternoon have different effects
