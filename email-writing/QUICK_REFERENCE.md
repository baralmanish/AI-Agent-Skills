## 🎯 Formality Scale

**1 = Very Formal** → "Dear [Name], I am writing to..."  
**2 = Professional** → "Hi [Name], I wanted to share..."  
**3 = Warm Pro** → "Hi [Name]! Hope you're doing well..."  
**4 = Casual** → "Hey [Name], Wanted to pick your brain..."  
**5 = Very Casual** → "Yo [Name], Quick question..."

---

## 😊 Emotional Tones

- **Urgent** — Deadline, immediate action needed
- **Grateful** — Thank yous, appreciation
- **Earnest** — Genuine, sincere interest
- **Enthusiastic** — Excited news, announcements
- **Neutral** — Informational, status updates
- **Empathetic** — Support, apologies, difficult news
- **Firm** — Boundaries, saying no, redirecting
- **Collaborative** — Partnership, teamwork

---

## ✅ Pre-Send Checklist

Before hitting send:

- [ ] Purpose is clear (why did I write this?)
- [ ] CTA is crystal clear (what do I want them to do?)
- [ ] Tone matches relationship + context
- [ ] I've acknowledged their perspective
- [ ] Tone is consistent throughout
- [ ] Grammar/spelling is clean
- [ ] I'd be happy to receive this email
- [ ] Email respects their time

---

## 🚨 Top 10 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Unclear CTA** — Reader doesn't know what to do
2. **Generic opening** — Could apply to anyone
3. **Burying the lede** — Important info is hidden
4. **Too many asks** — Pick one per email
5. **No deadline** — Reader doesn't know when you need a response
6. **Defensive apology** — Over-explaining instead of owning it
7. **Wrong formality** — Too formal with friend, too casual with boss
8. **All about you** — Doesn't acknowledge recipient's needs
9. **Wall of text** — No structure, hard to scan
10. **Vague proposal** — Unclear what you're actually proposing

## 💡 Context-Specific Tips

### Leadership Emails

- Lead with headline (status/result first)
- Use: Status | Wins | Challenges | Next Steps
- Include metrics
- 3-4 paragraphs max

### Networking

- Personalize specifically (show you know their work)
- Lead with value TO THEM (not what you want)
- Make specific ask (not vague)
- Make saying yes easy

### Apologies

- Lead with acknowledgment (not excuses)
- Take responsibility specifically
- Show you understand impact
- Offer concrete fix
- End on forward momentum

### Sales/Partnership

- Lead with THEIR problem (not your solution)
- Show you understand context
- Include proof points
- Clear ask (meeting? pilot?)
- Reduce perceived risk

## 📝 Email Structure Template

```
Subject: [Benefit/Urgency] + [Specific topic]

[Greeting]

[Opening - why this matters to THEM]

[Body - key message/context]
[If needed: support/proof points]

[CTA - crystal clear what you want]

[Closing]
```

## 🎯 One-Minute Decision Tree

**Who am I writing to?**

- Boss/Executive → Professional, concise, results-first
- Peer/Colleague → Warm-professional, conversational
- New Contact → Warm, specific, benefit-forward
- Customer → Empathetic, solution-focused
- Friend → Casual, genuine, personal

**What's the tone?**

- Asking for help → Earnest, specific, grateful
- Delivering news → Empathetic, clear, honest
- Announcing something → Enthusiastic, benefit-focused
- Building relationship → Warm, genuine, personal interest
- Setting boundary → Kind, clear, firm

**What's the CTA?**

- Response needed → Be specific (reply by X, questions welcome)
- Action needed → State it clearly (approve, attend, send)
- No response needed → Say so ("FYI, no response needed")

## ⚡ Pro Tips

✓ Short sentences beat long ones  
✓ One ask per email = higher response  
✓ CTA should be obvious  
✓ Warm beats perfect
