# Email Writing Skill - Template Examples

## Leadership/Executive Update Examples

### Template 1: Project Status Update

```
Subject: Q2 Project Status: On Track | Key Milestone Hit

Hi [Name],

We hit our key milestone this week and remain on track for our May 31 launch.

Status Summary:
• Engineering: 94% complete (on target for 95% by week's end)
• Design: 100% complete
• QA: In final testing (3 medium bugs found, 2 already resolved)
• One blocker: API performance under load. [Team] is engaged, resolution expected Wednesday.

Next Steps:
Full launch readiness review Thursday. Go-live Tuesday if QA clears.

I'll have a postmortem ready for you after launch.

Let me know if you'd like to dig into anything.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Template 2: Problem + Solution Update

```
Subject: Issue Found + Action Plan | [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

We discovered an issue with [specific thing] this week. Here's what we're doing.

What happened:
[Specific description without excuses]

Impact:
[Honest assessment of impact to business/timeline/customer]

Our action plan:
1. [Immediate mitigation] — [owner], done by [date]
2. [Root cause fix] — [owner], done by [date]
3. [Prevention] — [owner], done by [date]

This will delay [outcome] by [X] days. We're accelerating [other initiative] to offset.

This is on us to fix. I'll update you daily until resolved.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Networking/Outreach Examples

### Template: Cold Reach Out to Person You Admire

```
Subject: Your talk on AI agents really resonated with me

I listened to your keynote at [conference] on [topic] and one thing you said stuck 
with me: "[specific quote or idea]".

I'm building [your company/project] right now and we're tackling [related problem]. 
Your insight on [specific point] changed how I'm thinking about our approach.

I'd love 20 minutes of your perspective—especially on [specific question]. I suspect 
you've solved this before, and I'd learn a lot from your thinking.

Would you have time for a quick coffee chat in the next two weeks? I'm happy to work 
around your schedule.

Thanks for considering,
[Your name]
```

## Customer Support Examples

### Template: Problem + Solution

```
Subject: Re: Issue with [feature] — Here's what we're doing

Hi [Customer name],

Thanks for reporting that. I understand how frustrating [specific issue] must be, 
especially since [impact to their work].

Here's what happened and what we're doing about it:

What we found:
[Specific, honest explanation of the issue]

How we're fixing it:
1. Immediate workaround: [Clear workaround they can use today]
2. Permanent fix: Rolling out in our release on [date]
3. To make sure this doesn't happen again: [What we're implementing]

In the meantime, you can [specific workaround steps]. I'll check in with you Friday 
to make sure that's working.

I apologize for the trouble. Let me know if you have any other questions.

Best,
[Your name]
[Title]
[Contact info]
```

## Apology Examples

### Template: Apology for Missing Deadline

```
Subject: Apology — Missing [deadline]

Hi [Name],

I owe you an apology. I missed [deadline] and I didn't communicate with you about it 
until it was too late. That's on me.

Here's what happened:
[Honest explanation — no excuses, just facts]

Why that matters to you:
[Acknowledge the impact and inconvenience]

What I'm doing:
1. [Immediate mitigation] — done by [date]
2. [Process change to prevent this] — being implemented [when]
3. [How I'm going to keep you in the loop] — [specific frequency/method]

I understand the frustration. I'm committed to doing better.

Thanks for your patience.

[Your name]
```

## Decline/No Examples

### Template: Kind Decline to Help

```
Subject: Re: Project opportunity

Hi [Name],

I appreciate you thinking of me for this. It's exactly the kind of work I'd normally 
jump on, and I genuinely wish I had the capacity.

Here's my honest situation:
I'm at maximum load on [Current project] through [date]. Taking anything else on would 
mean doing both poorly, which isn't fair to you or me.

What I can do:
Option 1: I can introduce you to [Person] who would be perfect for this.
Option 2: I can pair with you for [specific limited help, X hours].
Option 3: I can prioritize this for [future date] when I have capacity.

Which would be most useful?

Thanks for understanding.

[Your name]
```

## Pro Tips for Each Context

### For Leadership Emails
✓ Lead with headline  
✓ Metrics before narrative  
✓ Be honest about problems + solutions  
✓ Respect their time (brief)

### For Outreach
✓ Personalize specifically  
✓ Lead with value to THEM  
✓ Make specific ask  
✓ Easy to say yes

### For Support
✓ Empathize first  
✓ Solution-focused  
✓ Clear next steps  
✓ Offer follow-up

### For Apologies
✓ Acknowledge upfront  
✓ Take responsibility  
✓ Show understanding of impact  
✓ Offer concrete fix

### For Sales
✓ Lead with their problem  
✓ Show you understand their context  
✓ Include proof points  
✓ Low-risk way to test

All templates are starting points—adapt tone and details to match your voice and their context!
